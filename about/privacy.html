Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy for Go Beyond Challenge

Last updated: April 2026

1. Introduction

Go Beyond Challenge Ltd (referred to as "we," "us," "our," or the "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website and when entering our events. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website and participate in our events.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

  • Event Entry: When you register for an event, we collect your name, email address, phone number, date of birth, address, emergency contact information, and any medical or dietary requirements you disclose.
  • Contact Forms: When you use our contact forms or email us directly, we collect the information you provide.
  • Website Usage: We may collect information about how you use our website, including pages visited, time spent on site, and referring URLs (via standard web analytics).
  • Payment Information: Payment data is processed by our payment provider and is not stored by us.
  • Cookies: We may use cookies to enhance your experience on our website.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal bases under GDPR:

  • Contract Performance: Processing necessary to provide event entry, registration, and related services.
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required for insurance, safety, and regulatory compliance.
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing to improve our services, communicate with you, and run our business effectively.
  • Consent: Where you have provided explicit consent, such as for marketing communications.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Processing your event entry and registration
  • Sending you confirmation details, updates, and race information
  • Contacting you in case of emergency
  • Insurance and safety purposes
  • Timing and results management
  • Communicating news, updates, and promotional information (with your consent)
  • Analyzing and improving our website and event services
  • Complying with legal and regulatory requirements

5. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your information with:

  • Service Providers: Third parties who provide services on our behalf, including timing systems, payment processors, and event management software.
  • Formspree: Our contact form processor, which handles form submissions securely.
  • Insurance Providers: Your information may be shared with our event insurance provider for coverage and claims purposes.
  • Medical Providers: Medical information may be shared with medical staff at the event only if necessary for your health and safety.
  • Legal Authorities: When required by law or to protect our legal rights.

6. Data Storage and Security

Your personal data is stored securely on encrypted servers with restricted access. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

We retain your event entry information for the duration of the event, plus two years thereafter for insurance and record-keeping purposes. Contact form submissions are retained for up to one year. You may request deletion of your data at any time, subject to legal obligations to retain records.

8. Your Rights Under GDPR

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access: You can request a copy of your personal data held by us.
  • Right to Rectification: You can request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: You can request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements).
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can request limitation on how we process your data.
  • Right to Data Portability: You can request a copy of your data in a portable format.
  • Right to Object: You can object to certain types of processing, including marketing communications.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You can lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe your rights have been violated.

9. Marketing Communications

We may send you marketing communications about our events and services if you have opted in. You can unsubscribe from marketing communications at any time by clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any email or by contacting us directly.

10. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites before providing your information.

11. Children's Privacy

Our events and website are not intended for children under 18 years old. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we become aware that a child has provided information to us, we will delete such information promptly.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will notify you of any material changes by updating the date at the top of this policy and, where appropriate, by posting a notice on our website.
